## Rationale

After analyzing 32 postings from ~2025-09 through 2026-05 for Junior/Associate/Entry-Level/I-grade AI infrastructure, MLOps, ML platform, and Junior DevOps-AI-platform roles, no requirement that the existing 10-module + 5-project level-10 curriculum does not already own has crossed the threshold of evidence to justify additive scope at level 10. The stable-core stack (Python, Linux, Git, Docker, Kubernetes, AWS/GCP, Prometheus/Grafana, CI/CD, Terraform basics, Flask/FastAPI, PostgreSQL, MLflow, Airflow/Prefect) covers >80% of explicit must-have requirements, and Modules 001-010 + Projects 01-05 already cover all of it. The two areas with material 2026 movement — LLM serving stacks (vLLM/Triton/TRT-LLM, in 6/32 postings) and vector DB / RAG plumbing (7/32) — show up consistently as preferred-not-required at the junior tier and are correctly owned at higher levels (ai-infra-engineer-learning level 20 owns LLM serving; ai-infra-ml-platform-learning level 30 owns RAG / vector DBs). Per the ownership rule and the explicit guidance to prefer zero additions over weakly-justified ones, no module, exercise, or project additions are proposed. JOB_REQUIREMENTS.md links to the higher-level owners for those requirements. The two partial-coverage notes (CI/CD pipelines for ML beyond capstone scope, and on-call lifecycle inside Module 009) are deliberately not promoted to modules because they each have <8 supporting postings and are naturally absorbed by existing project READMEs and existing module content, respectively — adding them would dilute, not strengthen, the level-10 scope.
